CAMP PIXELACHE 2013 (17-19.5.2013)
    
CALL FOR PRESENTATIONS & WORKSHOPS
DEADLINE: The call is open until 19.4 

We are calling out for short presentations (5-15mins) 
Our curated themes for the Festival are emerging as Creative Development (North-South, Tallinn-Helsinki  & other relations), Virtuality, Resonance, Anti-disciplinarity, Waste, Techno-ecologies, and Control. Welcome to be inspired by these or introduce other topics into the Camp!

Please note that for the workshops, apart from tables, limited electricity generated by solar cells and slow internet, Pixelache will not be able  to provide other infrastructure or materials that would require to be carried from the mainland. We would also like to stress that all  presentations and workshops should try to adapt their form and materiality to the given locality, in an attempt to create a symbiosis between the camp and the island. Keep this in mind when you think along your contribution!

Presentations can still be proposed by confirmed participants until the Camp Pixelache day, but because of the infrastructure on Naissaar and the fact that there is a limited amount of places and spaces, we need to  confirm all the participants, as well as workshops & demos well in  advance. The call is open until 19.4.

For other inquiries, please contact office [-at-] pixelache.ac

Two alternative workshop proposals. Needed: some kind of sound amplification. Can be adapted to the unique circumstances of the event, though.

I.
Workshop title:  Visual Beat Machines: Processing, Pd
Workshop description (length, for whom, how many participants, prior knowledge required?) : 

Even with no prior programming experience, learn to assemble your own simple music machines, with on-screen graphics and built-in sound, using entirely free tools. We'll see how basic sound synthesis and envelopes work, and add sequencing for melody and rhythm. On the visual side, we'll cover how to draw simple geometry and images, then add input from the mouse, data on the Internet, sensors, or anything else you might like. The result: audiovisual toys you can use on your Mac, Windows, or Linux computer.

Participants: recommend max 15
Prior knowledge required: none, though any background in Pd or Processing may be useful
Supplies to bring: a laptop (and if desired, any hardware you might like to interface with)


II. 
Workshop title: Making sound with the open source MeeBlip synthesizer
Workshop description (length, for whom, how many participants, prior knowledge required?) : 


Make bass-lines, melodies, and sounds with a full-featured, open source hardware synthesizer, the MeeBlip. Learn simple modifications to the MeeBlip's onboard code for hacking the synth. Then experiment with novel inputs by building a ribbon controller and using an infrared sensor. For those of you with a musical background and a MIDI interface, we will also learn the basics of MIDI and sequencing by controlling the synth with Pd, the free visual creation environment. But for anyone, we'll have an introduction to the world of synthesis and sound through open technology.

Prior knowledge: No previous programming experience needed - just a desire to make some noise.
Supplies: Bring a laptop and, if you have one, a MIDI interface. Provided for participants - MeeBlip micro, inputs and materials for ribbon controller. Pure Data and MeeBlip toolchain work on Mac, Windows, and Linux; free.
(Cost depends on included parts, but basic expenses for parts should be around 60 €.)
